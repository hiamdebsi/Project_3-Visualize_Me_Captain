Problem Analysis

Weather (Max Temp) correlates to the local HQ's of NYC stock exchange Close Price and Volume during Jan 2018 to Oct 2021.

A gentle breeze, a bit of rain... I don't mind this. Although, when it comes to a full out downpour of rain or a bad snow storm, this inclement weather may devastate a life, damage property, or close off an internet connection. Did some climatic event affect your financial situation? Let us investigate if daily weather has some affect on stock markets.

Considerations

Narrowing Our Scope: We will be considering weather in the New York city area, and stock exchanges from New York, where their head quarters are in NYC as well. Let's analyse the stock close prices and the transaction volumes to see if there is a pattern for different weather conditions and a correlation that may occur over time.

Data Extraction

Weather data was collected through an api from World Weather Online, from Jan 2014 to Nov 2021 for NYC. Historic stock information (until Nov 2021) was collected through an api from Alphavantage using their daily Times Series data and an api of Fundamental Data for their company overview. GPS coordinates were collected through Google Maps and Geopy.

Data Transformation

The CSV and JSON files from our extracts required standardization to ensure data would be able to be utilized for analysis.

Data Load

After standardizing the data, the data was loaded into a Mongo database.

RESULTS

Weather (Max Temp) correlates to the local HQ's of NYC stock exchange Close Price and Volume during Jan 2018 to Oct 2021.

Data Trends

The analyzer application is fairly straight forward to use. To view the trends you can go to the Analysis page and choose a stock (23 stocks are available, whose head quarters are in NYC). You can see a sinusoidal trend for the maximum temperature across time, which is justified by the change in seasons. As for the stock volume, a trend does not appear using the time series chart. In order to confirm any correlations, you can go to the correlations page to view the scatter charts.

Results: Correlation

For the results you can view the correlations page and look at the Pearson r-squared value. The user enters the stock symbol, and then you can choose if you want to compare daily maximum temperature with the stock volume or with the stock close price.

As you review the charts, you can notice some correlations by viewing the Pearson 'r squared' value. The daily maximum temperature presents a strong possibility of a correlation with the stock close prices. As well, there is a moderate possibility of a correlation with the maximum temperature and volume. However, these correlations may adjust slightly depending on the stock that you have chosen.

Interpretion Notes

The strength of a correlation between the Maximum Temperature and a stock index close price or transaction volume data is measured using the Pearson r-squared values. Here we are defining a Weak relationship: r2 < 0.5 , Moderate relationship r2< 0.7 and Strong relationship >0.7 Pearson's r-squared correlation uses a linear regression analysis. Some of the plots for the data appear non-linear and an alternative correlation method can be used for accuracy.

Map Notes

The application has an interactive map displaying the stock HQs of 23 stock markets within NYC. There is a zoom in feature which allows you to look into the map areas. When clicking on a particular marker, a popup shows up displaying the following information about the the HQ's; Stock Symbol, Full Company Name, HQ Address, Sector, Industry. This information is accurate as of Dec 2021.

Hypothesis

Weather (Max Temp) correlates to the local HQ's of NYC stock exchange Close Price and Volume during Jan 2018 to Oct 2021.

Based on the findings obtained we can not reject the null hypothesis declared of Weather (Max Temp) correlates to the local HQ's of NYC stock exchange Close Price and Volume during Jan 2018 to Oct 2021. When a regression analysis is performed, the daily maximum temperature presents a strong possibility of a correlation with the stock close prices (for IBM the r2= 0.96884). As well, there is a moderate possibility of a correlation with the maximum temperature and volume, with an r2 value of 0.52819 for IBM.
